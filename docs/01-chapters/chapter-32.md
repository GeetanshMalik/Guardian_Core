# Chapter 32 — Glossary

# 32.1 Introduction

This glossary defines the principal architectural, product, and engineering terminology used throughout the Guardian Core specification.

The objective is to ensure consistent interpretation of terminology across engineering, design, product, and future development activities.

Unless otherwise specified, the following definitions apply throughout this document.

---

# 32.2 Core Product Terms

### Guardian Core

The central autonomous intelligence platform responsible for planning, reasoning, learning, decision-making, and orchestration.

---

### Goal

A user-defined objective the platform attempts to help complete.

---

### Milestone

A significant intermediate achievement within a goal.

---

### Task

The smallest executable unit generated by the Planning Engine.

---

### Execution Graph

A directed graph representing dependencies and execution order for tasks and milestones.

---

### Goal Graph

The complete hierarchical representation of a user's objective.

---

### World State

The platform's current understanding of the user's environment, commitments, available resources, and execution context.

---

### Capability

An independent cognitive module responsible for a specialized responsibility such as planning or research.

---

### Orchestrator

The coordination layer responsible for selecting and sequencing capabilities.

---

### Shared Memory

Persistent knowledge accessible by every capability.

---

### Observation

An immutable fact captured from user behavior or system execution.

---

### Reflection

A structured evaluation generated after completing work.

---

### Preference

A learned user tendency supported by accumulated evidence.

---

### Research Package

A structured collection of curated resources prepared for a goal.

---

### Decision

A policy-approved autonomous recommendation or action.

---

### Clarification

Additional information requested when confidence is insufficient.

---

### Recovery Plan

An adaptive plan generated after schedule disruption.

---

### Daily Brief

An AI-generated summary of priorities, schedule, and recommendations.

---

# 32.3 AI & Cognitive Terms

### Cognitive Engine

The reasoning subsystem responsible for understanding user intent.

---

### Planning Engine

Generates executable strategies.

---

### Learning Engine

Transforms observations into long-term knowledge.

---

### Decision Engine

Evaluates actions against user policies and autonomy levels.

---

### Memory Retrieval

The process of retrieving relevant long-term knowledge for reasoning.

---

### Confidence Score

A quantitative estimate of certainty associated with reasoning or learning.

---

### Hallucination

AI-generated information that lacks factual support.

---

### Grounding

The process of validating AI reasoning using trusted context or external tools.

---

### Context Window

The amount of information available to the language model during inference.

---

# 32.4 System Architecture Terms

### Domain Event

An immutable record representing a completed business occurrence.

---

### Event Bus

Infrastructure responsible for transporting events between services.

---

### Worker

An independent background process performing asynchronous work.

---

### Repository

Persistence abstraction responsible for Firestore interactions.

---

### Adapter

A component translating between Guardian Core and external systems.

---

### API Gateway

The primary communication boundary between clients and backend services.

---

### DTO

Data Transfer Object exchanged through APIs.

---

### Idempotency

The property that repeated execution produces the same result.

---

### Correlation ID

Identifier connecting related events and requests.

---

### Dead Letter Queue (DLQ)

Storage for events that repeatedly fail processing.

---

### Infrastructure as Code (IaC)

Declarative management of infrastructure through version-controlled definitions.

---

### Blue-Green Deployment

Deployment strategy minimizing downtime through parallel environments.

---

### CI/CD

Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment pipeline.

---

# 32.5 Google Cloud Terms

### Cloud Run

Serverless execution environment for backend services.

---

### Firestore

Document-oriented database used for persistent storage.

---

### Gemini

Google's multimodal foundation model used for reasoning and planning.

---

### Secret Manager

Secure storage service for credentials and sensitive configuration.

---

### Cloud Scheduler

Managed scheduling service responsible for periodic execution.

---

### OAuth

Authorization protocol enabling secure access to Google services.

---

# 32.6 Product & User Terms

### Chief of Staff

The interaction metaphor representing Guardian Core as a trusted executive assistant.

---

### Autonomy Level

The degree of authority granted to Guardian Core for executing actions without additional confirmation.

---

### Productivity Score

Aggregate indicator representing execution effectiveness.

---

### Personalization

Adaptive modification of recommendations based on learned user preferences.

---

### Execution Risk

Estimated probability that a goal will miss its deadline.

---

### Focus Session

A dedicated scheduled period reserved for concentrated work.

---

### Productivity Pattern

A recurring behavioral trend detected through observations.

---

### Knowledge Package

A reusable collection of structured information supporting execution.

---

Throughout this specification, the following quality attributes are emphasized:

* Reliability
* Availability
* Maintainability
* Modularity
* Extensibility
* Explainability
* Observability
* Security
* Privacy
* Usability
* Performance
* Testability
* Portability
* Resilience

These attributes collectively define the engineering standards expected of Guardian Core.
